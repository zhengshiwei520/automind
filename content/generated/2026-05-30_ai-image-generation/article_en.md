## Section 7: Workflow Tips — Getting the Best Results

### 1. Master prompt engineering (even with natural language)
While 2026 models understand plain English better than ever, specificity still wins. Instead of "a cat sitting on a chair," try "a fluffy orange tabby cat lounging on a mid-century modern armchair, soft morning light through sheer curtains, shallow depth of field, warm color palette."

### 2. Leverage Canva's Brand Kit for consistency
If you're using Canva AI (and seriously, you should be), set up your Brand Kit once. Upload your logo, define your color palette (including primary, secondary, and accent colors), and add your brand fonts. Every AI generation will automatically align with your brand guidelines — no manual adjustments needed.

### 3. Combine tools in a pipeline
The smartest creators don't pick one tool. They build a workflow:
- **Ideation:** Midjourney for creative exploration
- **Refinement:** Canva AI for layout, text, and polish
- **Delivery:** Canva for resizing and export

This pipeline gives you Midjourney's artistic quality with Canva's production efficiency.

### 4. Use negative prompts wisely
All five platforms support negative prompts. Common ones: "no text, no watermark, no low quality, no ugly, no distorted proportions." For product shots, add "no reflections, no shadows" or "clean background, minimalist."

### 5. Batch generate and curate
Don't settle for the first result. Generate 8–16 variations, then select the best 2–3 for refinement. Canva AI's batch mode makes this fast — you can generate 16 images in under 10 seconds and pick your favorites.

### 6. Upscale with care
Modern generators produce high native resolutions, but if you need extreme detail, use the platform's built-in upscaler rather than third-party tools. Canva AI's "Magic Upscale" (included in Pro) adds detail rather than just stretching pixels.

### 7. Stay on top of style trends
AI image generators in 2026 support style references. If you see a visual style you like, save it as a reference image and feed it into your generator as a style prompt. Canva AI lets you upload up to 5 reference images per generation for multi-style fusion.
