## Automation Workflows Every Small Business Needs

Regardless of which platform you choose, these six automations will drive the majority of your email revenue:

### 1. The Welcome Sequence (5–7 emails)
The moment someone subscribes, they should receive:
- Email 1: Immediate delivery of the lead magnet + a personal note from you
- Email 2: "Who I am and why I started this" — build trust
- Email 3: Your most popular free resource
- Email 4: A customer story or case study
- Email 5: A low-risk offer (e.g., $10 ebook or $47 mini-course)
- Email 6: Social proof + a more serious offer
- Email 7: "Last chance" for the offer with a deadline

Open rates for welcome sequences average 50–70%. This is where subscribers decide whether to trust you.

### 2. Abandoned Cart (Ecommerce)
Trigger 1 hour after abandonment: "Did something go wrong?" Include a direct link back to the cart. Follow up 24 hours later with social proof (reviews of the product). Send a final email at 72 hours with a small discount if needed. This single automation recovers 10–15% of lost revenue.

### 3. Educational Drip (5–10 emails over 2–4 weeks)
Send value-first content that solves one specific problem. No offers until email 5 or 6. This builds authority and primes subscribers for future purchases.

### 4. Post-Purchase Upsell
3 days after purchase: "Here's how to get the most out of [product]." 7 days: "You might also like [related product]." 30 days: "Share your experience and get [incentive]."

### 5. Re-engagement (Win-Back)
Subscribers inactive for 90 days get a special email: "We miss you — here's something free." If no open in 7 days, send one last email: "Should we say goodbye?" Unsubscribe anyone who doesn't engage. A smaller, engaged list beats a bloated, disengaged one every time.

### 6. Birthday/Anniversary
Simple but effective. "Happy birthday — here's 20% off your next order." People love feeling remembered.

## FAQs

**What's the best email marketing tool for a complete beginner in 2026?**

ConvertKit or MailerLite. ConvertKit if you plan to sell digital products or build an audience long-term. MailerLite if you want the cheapest option with a great editor. Avoid Mailchimp unless you really need the free plan to test the waters.

**How many subscribers do I need before email marketing makes sense?**

As few as 50. Even a handful of engaged subscribers can generate sales if you send the right offers. Start building your list from day one — don't wait until you have "enough" people.

**Is ConvertKit really worth the price compared to MailerLite?**

For most small businesses, yes. ConvertKit's tag-based system, superior automation, and creator-focused features save you hours of manual work each week. That time savings alone justifies the price difference. Plus, the affiliate program (30% recurring for 2 years) can offset costs if you recommend it to others.

**Can I migrate from Mailchimp to ConvertKit easily?**

Yes. ConvertKit offers a free migration service for paid plans. They'll move your subscribers, tags, and forms. Most migrations complete within 48 hours.

**Do I need a separate landing page builder?**

Not with ConvertKit or MailerLite — both have capable built-in landing page builders. ActiveCampaign and Brevo also include them. Mailchimp's free plan limits landing pages, so you might need a third-party tool there.

**What email marketing tool is best for a physical products store?**

ConvertKit (for relationship emails + Shopify integration) combined with Brevo (for transactional emails like order confirmations) is a powerful combo. Or use ConvertKit alone — it handles transactional-style emails well through manual automations.

**How do I improve email deliverability?**

Use a dedicated sending domain, warm up your sending reputation, clean your list regularly (remove bounces and unengaged subscribers), avoid spam trigger words in subject lines, and use a platform with strong deliverability like ConvertKit or MailerLite.

**What's the difference between tags and segments?**

Tags are labels you apply to individual subscribers (e.g., "purchased-ebook"). Segments are dynamic groups based on conditions (e.g., "all subscribers who opened email in the last 30 days"). ConvertKit uses tags as its primary organizational method, while other platforms rely more on segments. Tags are more flexible for automation triggers.

**Can I send automated birthday or anniversary emails?**

Yes, in every platform listed. ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign, and MailerLite make it easy with date-based triggers. Mailchimp requires a paid plan for this feature.
